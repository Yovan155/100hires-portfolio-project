## Recent LinkedIn Content Analysis / Blog Content Analysis

*(Fishkin publishes primarily via the SparkToro blog, which functions as his LinkedIn-equivalent long-form channel; content below is drawn from that channel.)*

**1. "In 2026, Less Than One Third of Google Searches Still Send a Click" (SparkToro, June 8, 2026)**
- *Core Insight:* Using Similarweb clickstream data, Fishkin found 68.01% of US Google searches in the first four months of 2026 ended without any click — not just no click to your site, no click anywhere. That's up from 60.45% in 2024 (Datos data) and roughly 45% a decade ago. Notably, AI Mode itself accounted for only 0.34% of searches in the study window, meaning AI Overviews and on-SERP answer features — not conversational AI Mode — are the primary current driver.
- *Why It Matters:* This kills the assumption that "AI Mode is the threat to watch" — the bigger, already-arrived threat is AI Overviews and answer-style SERP features absorbing clicks today.
- *Application for B2B SaaS:* Stop sizing AI-search risk by AI Mode adoption numbers; size it by AI Overview trigger rate on your target queries, since that's where the click loss is actually happening right now.

**2. "5 Strategic Features that Predict Survival in the Zero-Click Era" (SparkToro, April 20, 2026)**
- *Core Insight:* Citing Cyrus Shepard's analysis of 400 websites that did *not* collapse during the 2024–2026 "traffic apocalypse," Fishkin argues tactical excellence (best content, best keyword research, best social editing) does not predict survival. What predicts survival is whether the business model itself is something Google/AI can disintermediate. Sites built around things AI can't easily replace (e.g., transactional infrastructure, proprietary data, community) outperformed those built purely on informational content.
- *Why It Matters:* This reframes "are we losing traffic" as the wrong question; the right question is "is our business model disintermediation-resistant."
- *Application for B2B SaaS:: Audit which parts of your funnel are purely informational (replaceable by an AI answer) versus which require visiting your product (demos, trials, interactive tools, community) — and weight content investment toward the latter.

**3. "Can you even track AI visibility?" — SparkToro Office Hours recap (March 11, 2026)**
- *Core Insight:* Fishkin's research found AI responses are highly inconsistent at the individual-query level when recommending brands or products, but brand frequency/presence can be measured statistically across large samples of repeated queries.
- *Why It Matters:* Many vendors sell "AI visibility tracking" as if a single AI Overview snapshot is meaningful; Fishkin's data says you need volume and statistical aggregation to draw any real conclusion.
- *Application for B2B SaaS:: Don't react to single AI-answer snapshots in Slack ("we're not mentioned by ChatGPT for X!"). Build a repeated-sampling measurement process before drawing strategic conclusions.

## B2B SaaS Application

- **Team Workflows:** Add a "business model disintermediation" review to the content roadmap process — for each planned content investment, ask whether it could be answered entirely inside an AI surface without a site visit.
- **Content Operations:** Reallocate budget from high-volume informational content toward interactive, demo-able, or community-dependent assets that can't be fully disintermediated.
- **SEO Systems:** Track AI Overview trigger rate on your specific target queries (not industry-wide AI Mode adoption) as your primary AI-search risk metric.
- **AI Usage:** Use AI-visibility tools only if they sample repeatedly across many queries/sessions; distrust single-snapshot "are we mentioned" checks.
- **Growth Opportunity:** Apply the 80/20 principle explicitly: pick the 1–2 channels where your B2B audience demonstrably pays attention and is reachable, rather than spreading content thin across every new AI-search surface.

## Actionable Takeaways

1. Replace "AI Mode adoption" as your AI-search risk metric with "AI Overview trigger rate on our target queries" — that's where today's actual click loss originates.
2. Run a disintermediation audit on your content roadmap: which planned pieces could be fully answered by an AI surface with no site visit, and deprioritize pure-informational investment there.
3. Build AI-visibility measurement on repeated sampling across many queries, not single-snapshot checks of "are we mentioned."
4. Apply an explicit 80/20 filter to channel investment — concentrate, don't spread, across emerging AI-search surfaces.
5. Start reporting a "Zero Click Marketing" line item separate from SEO traffic — measuring brand mentions, community presence, and earned visibility that doesn't depend on a click.
