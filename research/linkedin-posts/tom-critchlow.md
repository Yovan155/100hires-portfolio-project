## Recent LinkedIn Content Analysis

*(Critchlow's primary long-form channel is Substack/his personal site; the insights below are drawn from his most recent published essays and newsletter, which function as his core thought-leadership content.)*

**1. "Why AI Search Matters" (The SEO MBA, April 28, 2026)**
- *Core Insight:* For B2B companies specifically, Critchlow frames AI search as a "shortlist and sentiment" problem, not a traffic problem: the real question is whether your brand is mentioned in the right contexts, described accurately, and recommended for the right use cases — and self-promoting listicles have already stopped working as a way to influence that. He also notes the craft layer of SEO (rank tracking, crawling, content production, reporting, forecasting, tooling) is being rebuilt in real time.
- *Why It Matters:* It reframes the entire AI-search conversation for B2B away from "are we visible" toward "are we accurately and favorably positioned" — a sentiment and messaging problem as much as a technical one.
- *Application for B2B SaaS:: Run periodic "sentiment audits" — sample how AI tools describe your product, category position, and ideal use case, and treat inaccurate or unfavorable framing as a content/PR problem to actively correct, not a technical SEO ticket.

**2. "Leveling Up SEO for the AI Era" interview content / "uncomfortable truths" framing**
- *Core Insight:* Critchlow's central, repeated claim: the real wins in the AI search era increasingly come from brand, product, and PR — not classic SEO tactics. SEO's enduring value is in *diagnosis* (understanding why something is happening), while actually fixing outcomes increasingly requires product, brand, and PR teams to act on that diagnosis.
- *Why It Matters:* This is a structural argument for embedding SEO inside cross-functional growth teams rather than running it as an isolated content/technical function.
- *Application for B2B SaaS:: Position the SEO function as the team that *diagnoses* visibility and sentiment problems and routes the fix to the right owner (product, brand, PR, content) rather than expecting SEO alone to solve every visibility gap.

**3. "How to Make an SEO Strategy" (The SEO MBA archive, recurring reference piece)**
- *Core Insight:* A good SEO strategy is fundamentally a communication exercise. Critchlow's method: state a clear diagnosis ("traffic is down 20% YoY because competitors are outranking us on core commercial queries with better pages and faster link velocity"), then a guiding policy in plain language with no jargon ("we will invest in improving core category pages and increase link-building"), understandable across the whole organization.
- *Why It Matters:* Most SEO strategy documents fail not because the tactics are wrong but because they're illegible to the executives who must fund them.
- *Application for B2B SaaS:: Rewrite your next SEO/content strategy doc in the diagnosis → guiding-policy format, stripped of SEO jargon, before it goes anywhere near a budget conversation.

## Video / Podcast / Webinar Analysis

**1. "The Search Session" — Tom Critchlow on "Leveling Up SEO for the AI Era" w/ Gianluca Fiorelli**
- *Platform:* Advanced Web Ranking podcast/video series
- *Date:* October 2025
- *Summary:* Critchlow and Fiorelli discuss the "power of linking out" (AI search is currently stuck producing wall-of-text answers, and linking out to sources restores real user value), the promise of Google's "Web Guide" feature (a more link-rich, structured blend of classic results with AI synthesis), and Critchlow's view that real competitive wins now come from brand, product, and PR rather than classic SEO tactics alone.
- *Key Takeaways:* AI Mode/AI Overview formats are still evolving toward more link-rich, attributable answers (Web Guide as an example), but are currently too slow for mainstream rollout; SEO's place at scale is increasingly fragmenting into multiple specialties living inside product, brand, and growth functions rather than one unified "SEO team."
- *Practical Applications:: Watch Google's "Web Guide" experiment closely as a leading indicator of where AI Overviews are heading (more attributable, link-rich answers) and prepare content to be cleanly excerptable/linkable in that format.

**2. The SEO MBA — "Good Slides Reduce Complexity" / executive-presence body of work**
- *Platform:* Newsletter (newsletter.seomba.com), referenced retrospectively in "The Best of the SEO MBA"
- *Summary:* Across his executive-presence writing, Critchlow teaches SEO professionals to present using a pyramid-principle structure: lead with the recommendation, then the supporting diagnosis, stripped of technical jargon, scaled to the size of the ask being made.
- *Key Takeaways:* "Selling SEO as growth, which is infinite, rather than optimization, which sounds marginal" is repeatedly cited by his own audience as the most useful single reframing in his body of work.
- *Practical Applications:: When pitching AI-search/SEO investment internally, frame it explicitly as a growth investment with open-ended upside, not as an "optimization" or maintenance cost line item.

## B2B SaaS Application

- **Team Workflows:** Position SEO as a cross-functional diagnostic function that routes fixes to product, brand, or PR rather than owning every fix itself.
- **Content Operations:** Require every content/SEO strategy document to follow the diagnosis → guiding-policy structure before it's pitched for budget.
- **SEO Systems:** Add a recurring AI-sentiment audit (how do AI tools describe us, our category position, our ideal use case) to the existing rank-tracking cadence.
- **AI Usage:** Use AI for the "craft layer" (tracking, crawling, reporting, forecasting) to free strategic time, while keeping diagnosis and prioritization a human/strategic function.
- **Growth Opportunity:** Reframe SEO/AI-search investment internally as "growth" (open-ended upside) rather than "optimization" (marginal, easily cut) to protect and grow budget.
