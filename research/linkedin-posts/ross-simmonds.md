## Recent LinkedIn Content (Minimum 3 Examples)

### 1. B2B Backlink Intelligence Report (Foundation Marketing company page)
- **Post Topic:** Original research analyzing 12,154 pages across 24 B2B brands and 2.4 million referring domains to determine which content formats actually earn backlinks.
- **Main Insight:** The content format most B2B teams over-invest in is one of the weakest backlink earners in the dataset, while an under-invested format earns roughly 4.25x its "fair share" of links.
- **Practical Application:** B2B SaaS content teams should audit their content mix against actual link-earning performance data (by format and by vertical — SaaS vs. consulting require different playbooks) before locking in the next quarter's editorial calendar.
- **Why It Matters for B2B SaaS:** Most SaaS content teams allocate budget based on intuition or competitor mimicry. Data-driven format allocation is a direct lever for authority-building, which increasingly correlates with AI citation frequency, not just rankings.

### 2. Reddit Dominance in B2B SaaS SERPs (Foundation Marketing + collaboration with Ty Magnin)
- **Post Topic:** Analysis of how often Reddit ranks for high-intent, solution-aware B2B SaaS keywords across 250 verticals.
- **Main Insight:** Reddit is no longer a B2C-only visibility channel — it ranks prominently for "best software" and comparison-style queries, and its content is increasingly surfaced inside LLM answers as a trusted, community-vetted source.
- **Practical Application:** B2B SaaS marketers should treat Reddit as a structured distribution channel: identify which subreddits already rank for category keywords, contribute genuinely useful answers, and avoid manufactured promotion (the post cites a competitor being banned from key subreddits for gaming the system).
- **Why It Matters for B2B SaaS:** Both traditional Google SERPs and AI answer engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, AI Overviews) weight community consensus heavily. Ignoring Reddit means ceding a growing share of both organic and AI-driven discovery to competitors.

### 3. Content Automation Philosophy (personal LinkedIn)
- **Post Topic:** A direct statement on what AI content automation is — and isn't — for marketing teams.
- **Main Insight:** Content automation should remove slow, repetitive production work (drafting, formatting, reformatting for channels), not replace the strategic and creative judgment of the marketer.
- **Practical Application:** SaaS content teams should map their workflow and identify the specific repetitive sub-tasks (first drafts, channel reformatting, distribution scheduling) suitable for AI/automation tooling like Distribution.ai, while keeping ideation, narrative, and quality control human-led.
- **Why It Matters for B2B SaaS: This reframes the AI-content debate away from "replace humans" toward "remove bottlenecks," which is the model most growth-stage SaaS teams need to scale output without sacrificing the differentiated point of view that earns AI citations.

### 4. "CREAM is Dead, DREAM Wins" framework teaser (cross-posted from his podcast)
- **Post Topic:** A reframing of the classic "Content Rules Everything Around Me" mantra for the AI search era.
- **Main Insight:** Distribution — not content volume — is what compounds into AI visibility, because LLMs cite brands that show up consistently across many trusted surfaces over years, not single optimized pages.
- **Practical Application:** Build a "Distribution Rules Everything Around Me" (DREAM) operating cadence: every piece of core content should be mapped to a deliberate multi-surface distribution plan (Reddit, YouTube, review sites, guest content, newsletters) at the time of creation, not as an afterthought.
- **Why It Matters for B2B SaaS:** SaaS buying committees increasingly research vendors through AI assistants before ever visiting a website. Brands with broad, multi-surface evidence trails get cited; brands that only optimize their own domain do not.

## Strategic Takeaway for B2B SaaS Growth Teams

Ross Simmonds' LinkedIn presence consistently reinforces one structural idea: **content creation and content distribution are two separate disciplines, and B2B SaaS teams chronically under-invest in the second.** As AI search rewards brands with the broadest "trust footprint" across the web (not just the strongest single domain), this distribution-first philosophy is becoming a prerequisite for AI visibility, not just an organic growth nice-to-have.
