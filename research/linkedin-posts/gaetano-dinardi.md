## Recent LinkedIn Content (Minimum 3 Examples)

### 1. The 2026 HubSpot AEO Playbook Announcement
- **Post Topic:** Launch of a practical 90-day system for HubSpot users moving from traditional SEO to Answer Engine Optimization (AEO).
- **Main Insight:** Buying committees are now using AI tools (e.g., Gemini) to pressure-test vendor proposals before ever replying to sales — meaning a brand's website is no longer competing for rankings, it is competing to *become the answer* AI gives during evaluation.
- **Practical Application:** Build a week-by-week 90-day roadmap that addresses three things simultaneously: (1) the content/video/entity signals AI systems trust, (2) consideration-and-decision-stage content (not just top-of-funnel), and (3) measurement that accounts for fewer clicks but higher-quality deals.
- **Why It Matters for B2B SaaS:** This directly addresses a fear many SaaS CMOs have: that AI tools are now part of the buying committee's due diligence process, invisible to traditional analytics, but highly influential on deal outcomes.

### 2. "Dark Social → Dark SEO" (LinkedIn Pulse article)
- **Post Topic:** A framework for how brands earn AI citations through indirect, hard-to-track ("dark") signals rather than direct optimization.
- **Main Insight:** AI models cite brands based on "information gain" — unique data, contrarian points of view, and proprietary research — and they validate that information by checking whether a brand is consistently mentioned across review sites (G2, Capterra), communities (Reddit, Quora), and third-party publishers.
- **Practical Application:** Build a structured "AI answer" content bucket strategy: publish proprietary research/data (the strongest citation bait), publish a clearly-stated contrarian or unique point of view, and publish reproducible tactical blueprints — and treat consistent mentions across review sites and communities as a measurable distribution objective, not an afterthought.
- **Why It Matters for B2B SaaS:** SaaS categories are crowded with generic, interchangeable content. Gaetano's framework gives a concrete prioritization test for "is this worth publishing" based on whether AI would actually have a reason to cite it.

### 3. Three Content Buckets That Get Cited by AI (carousel/post breakdown)
- **Post Topic:** A direct breakdown of the three content types AI search rewards: Unique Insight, Evidentiary Data, and Tactical Blueprint.
- **Main Insight:** Generic, rehashed content is actively filtered out by AI systems looking for grounding material — if a blog post just repeats common knowledge, the AI will cite the competitor who "did the work" (original data, original framework, or a clearly structured how-to).
- **Practical Application:** Run existing content libraries through this 3-bucket filter; any piece that doesn't fall into Unique Insight, Evidentiary Data, or Tactical Blueprint should either be cut, consolidated, or upgraded with original data/POV before being considered for AI-citation-focused promotion.
- **Why It Matters for B2B SaaS:** Most B2B content libraries are bloated with "what is X" filler. This is a practical content-audit framework SaaS teams can apply immediately without new tooling.

### 4. PageRank/E-E-A-T Technical Deep Dive
- **Post Topic:** A technical correction and explainer on how PageRank actually works, tying it to E-E-A-T and canonicalization signals.
- **Main Insight:** Many SEO tools and practitioners still operate on a misunderstood or outdated version of the PageRank formula, which leads to misallocated link-building effort.
- **Practical Application:** SaaS SEO teams should audit the technical assumptions baked into their SEO tooling/vendor stack rather than assuming default settings and reports reflect how search engines actually compute authority today.
- **Why It Matters for B2B SaaS:** Technical SEO misunderstandings compound at scale — a flawed mental model applied across hundreds of programmatic or product pages can quietly waste significant content and link-building budget.
