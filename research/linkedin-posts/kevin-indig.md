## Recent LinkedIn / Growth Memo Content (Minimum 3 Examples)

### 1. "The Ghost Citation Problem"
- **Post Topic:** Original research across 3,981 domains, 115 prompts, 14 countries, and 4 AI search engines examining when and why brands get cited (or not) in AI answers.
- **Main Insight:** Citation behavior in AI search is inconsistent and engine-specific — a brand can appear dominant in an aggregated AI-visibility dashboard while being effectively invisible in two of the three major engines measured individually.
- **Practical Application:** SaaS marketing teams should stop relying on a single blended "AI visibility score" from monitoring tools and instead break out citation performance by individual engine (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Mode, Gemini) to identify where real exposure gaps exist.
- **Why It Matters for B2B SaaS:** Budget and roadmap decisions based on an inflated aggregate score can create false confidence — a SaaS brand might think it's "winning" in AI search while actually losing ground in the specific engine its buyers use most.

### 2. "Growth Intelligence Brief #12" — AI Visibility Hype vs. Search Ecosystem Reality
- **Post Topic:** A grounded analysis of whether AI is "collapsing" search, using market-cap and revenue-breakeven analysis (citing Aswath Damodaran's valuation framework for AI labs) alongside search-behavior data.
- **Main Insight:** AI is stressing the search ecosystem unevenly rather than replacing it outright — Google still captures the majority of discovery and ad dollars, while only specific high-intent query types are shifting to AI-native interfaces.
- **Practical Application:** Marketing leaders should resist the urge to over-rotate budget toward "AI visibility tools" purely because of hype; instead, they should evaluate whether a given AI-visibility product addresses a genuine measurement gap, and communicate measured caution internally rather than either dismissing or overselling the shift.
- **Why It Matters for B2B SaaS:** Many SaaS marketing orgs are under pressure from leadership to "do something about AI search" immediately. Kevin's framing gives growth leaders a defensible, data-informed way to prioritize without chasing every new tool.

### 3. "Intent Compression" and the Death of 20 Years of SEO Segmentation
- **Post Topic:** How AI's ability to synthesize and compress multiple search intents into a single answer breaks the keyword-by-intent content mapping model SEO has run on for two decades.
- **Main Insight:** Per-page, per-keyword optimization is losing relevance because AI systems collapse what used to be dozens of separate searches into one compressed exchange — but topical content strategy (mapping a market, not a keyword list) survives and becomes more important.
- **Practical Application:** B2B SaaS content teams should shift planning from keyword-by-keyword content calendars toward topic/market maps that demonstrate comprehensive coverage and point-of-view across a category, since that's what AI systems draw from when compressing multiple intents into one answer.
- **Why It Matters for B2B SaaS:** SaaS categories are often narrow and technical; topical authority (not keyword count) is what allows a mid-market vendor to be cited as an authority alongside category leaders.

### 4. SEO Job Market Data — AI Skills Repricing Roles
- **Post Topic:** Analysis (with Amanda Johnson) of how the SEO job market has quietly repriced around AI skills, even though most new higher-paying roles don't explicitly say "AI" in the title.
- **Main Insight:** AI fluency is now an embedded expectation inside SEO hiring and compensation, not a separate specialization — four out of five of the highest-paying new roles assume AI literacy without naming it.
- **Practical Application:** SaaS growth leaders building or restructuring content/SEO teams should evaluate candidates on applied AI workflow fluency (prompting, AI-assisted research, AI content QA) as a baseline skill, not a "nice to have," and adjust job descriptions and comp bands accordingly.
- **Why It Matters for B2B SaaS:** Hiring and team-structure decisions lag market reality. This data gives B2B SaaS hiring managers a benchmark for what "AI-ready" content/SEO talent actually looks like in compensation terms.
