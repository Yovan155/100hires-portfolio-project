## Recent LinkedIn Content Analysis

**1. "Enterprise SEO that actually scales in 2026" — practical playbook**
- *Core Insight:* Scaling enterprise SEO requires aligning marketing, product, and tech under one operating model, centralizing data/governance while giving local/regional teams clear SLAs, and forming modular cross-functional squads that own outcomes end-to-end.
- *Why It Matters:* Most SEO scaling failures are organizational, not technical — without a single owner per domain, duplicated and conflicting work creeps in as teams grow.
- *Application for B2B SaaS:* If a SaaS company is expanding content across multiple product lines or markets, define one accountable owner per topical/product domain before adding headcount or tooling.

**2. "How to Build a Representative AI Search Prompt Library" (LinkedIn, June 8, 2026)**
- *Core Insight:* Most teams measuring AI search visibility use whatever default prompt list a tool provides, which produces invalid data — you end up measuring presence for a random sample of prompts unrelated to your actual buyers' journeys. Solis's fix: build prompt sets that are representative (mapped to real audience/journey stages), structured (organized by topic/audience/journey), realistic (real user language), and repeatable (run on a fixed cadence) — and keep separate prompt sets for core tracking, experiments, and ongoing monitoring.
- *Why It Matters:* AI-visibility reporting is becoming a board-level metric; if the underlying prompt set is wrong, every downstream decision (content prioritization, "wins," budget asks) is built on noise.
- *Application for B2B SaaS:* Before investing in any AI-visibility tracking tool, build the prompt library first — sourced from real sales-call language, support tickets, and customer interviews — then map each visibility gap to one of three causes: a content gap, a source-credibility issue, or an entity-recognition problem, since each requires a different fix.

**3. "A 3-Layer Framework to Measure AI Presence, Readiness, and Business Impact" (aleydasolis.com, updated June 19, 2026)**
- *Core Insight:* The old measurement model (rankings, clicks, sessions) is no longer sufficient on its own. Solis proposes layering AI *presence* (do you show up), AI *readiness* (can your content/structure actually be retrieved and cited), and *business impact* (does presence translate to pipeline) as three distinct measurement layers.
- *Why It Matters:* Teams often conflate "we appear in AI answers" with "this is driving revenue" — the framework forces a more honest, staged measurement model.
- *Application for B2B SaaS:* Build dashboards in three explicit tiers rather than one blended AI-visibility score, and report each tier separately to leadership so wins aren't overstated.

## Video / Podcast / Webinar Analysis

**1. "Mastering AI Search: Insights from Aleyda Solís on SEO" — Humans of MarTech podcast**
- *Platform:* Apple/Spotify/YouTube podcast feed (humansofmartech.com)
- *Date:* January 13, 2026
- *Summary:* Solis explains how AI crawlers behave differently than traditional search crawlers — she has seen brands accidentally block AI crawlers, or hide entire content sections behind JavaScript rendering that AI systems never see. She frames discovery as now happening across Google, TikTok, Instagram, and LLMs simultaneously, requiring teams to treat discovery as a multi-platform system rather than a single-channel SEO program.
- *Key Takeaways:* Crawlability fundamentals (robots.txt, JS rendering, server response to AI user-agents) are being silently broken at many brands without anyone noticing; authority signals now extend to forums, newsletters, podcasts, and social communities, not just backlinks.
- *Practical Applications:* Run an AI-crawler-specific technical audit (not just a Googlebot audit) — verify what GPTBot, PerplexityBot, and other AI crawlers can actually retrieve from key pages, including JS-rendered content.

**2. "The New Rules of AI Visibility and How to Prepare for It" — Moz webinar / guide**
- *Platform:* Moz (webinar + companion written guide)
- *Summary:* Solis lays out the practical risk that brands face when their AI visibility is contingent on factors outside classic on-page SEO — crawler access, third-party corroboration, and structured data/schema — and previews her "3-Layer" measurement approach.
- *Key Takeaways:* Schema and structured data matter more for AI search than they ever did for classic SEO, because they reduce ambiguity for retrieval systems with no human judgment in the loop.
- *Practical Applications:* Prioritize schema markup (Organization, Product, FAQ, Article) as a 2026 technical SEO baseline, not an optional enhancement — treat it as part of the AI "readiness" layer.

## AI-Powered SEO Frameworks

- **AI Content Production Workflow:** N/A as a standalone content-writing system — Solis's contribution is upstream of content production: prompt-library and measurement design that should *inform* what gets written.
- **SEO Automation Process:** AI-crawler-specific technical audits run on a recurring cadence (separate from standard Googlebot crawl audits).
- **Content Scaling System:** Centralized governance + federated regional/product squads, each with clear SLAs and a single domain owner — the operating model that makes scaled content production sustainable across markets.
- **Topical Authority Approach:** Map prompt libraries to real customer journeys/topics, then identify content gaps against that map rather than against keyword volume alone.
- **Content Refresh Strategy:** Re-run the AI prompt library on a scheduled cadence and use citation/accuracy drift over time (not just presence/absence) as the trigger for refresh.
- **Search Visibility Tactic:** Diagnose every AI-visibility gap as one of three categories — content gap, source-credibility issue, or entity-recognition issue — because the fix differs by category.

## B2B SaaS Application

- **Team Workflows:** Assign one accountable owner per product/topical domain before scaling regional or multi-product content efforts; avoid duplicated, conflicting SEO work across teams.
- **Content Operations:** Build a real, sales/support-sourced AI prompt library before buying or trusting any AI-visibility tracking tool's default prompt set.
- **SEO Systems:** Separate AI crawler audits from standard technical SEO audits; verify GPTBot/PerplexityBot/ClaudeBot access explicitly, especially for JS-heavy product pages.
- **AI Usage:** Use AI-visibility data diagnostically (which of the 3 gap categories applies) rather than as a vanity scoreboard.
- **Growth Opportunity:** Schema/structured data investment as a fast, high-leverage technical project that improves both classic SEO and AI retrieval readiness simultaneously.

## Actionable Takeaways

1. Build a real AI search prompt library sourced from sales calls, support tickets, and customer interviews before trusting any AI-visibility tool's defaults.
2. Run a dedicated AI-crawler technical audit (GPTBot, PerplexityBot, etc.) separate from your standard Googlebot crawl audit, with special attention to JS-rendered content.
3. Assign a single accountable owner per product/topical domain before scaling content across regions or product lines.
4. Report AI visibility in three distinct tiers — presence, readiness, business impact — instead of one blended score.
5. Treat schema/structured data as 2026 technical SEO baseline infrastructure, not an optional add-on.
