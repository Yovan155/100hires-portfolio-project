## Title
The Future of Search Isn't Search

## Source
Marketing Speak Podcast

## Date
~March 2026

## Summary
Kevin Indig discusses findings from his original user research into Google's AI Overviews, including a striking behavioral shift: desktop click-through rates on traditional search results dropped from 28% to just 11% in monitored sessions. He highlights a generational divide — younger users readily accept AI-generated overviews as sufficient answers, while older users still default to clicking through to source sites. Kevin argues that trust, not relevance, is now the primary filter users apply before engaging with any result, and that brands need to build recognition and trust *before* a user searches — closing the gap between social media presence and SEO performance. He also reframes AI as most valuable as a "thought partner" for strategic thinking, not merely a task-execution tool.

## Key Lessons
1. **CTR collapse is measurable and generational.** Desktop CTR dropping from 28% to 11% in AI Overview sessions means SaaS marketers must redefine what "success" looks like in organic search — visibility and trust now matter more than click volume.
2. **Trust precedes relevance as a ranking filter in the user's mind.** If a brand isn't already recognizable when a buyer encounters it in an AI summary, even a relevant, accurate answer may not convert attention.
3. **Social media and SEO need to be operationally bridged.** Pre-search brand exposure (via paid and organic social) is what makes a brand "recognizable" when it later appears inside an AI Overview or search result — these channels can no longer be planned in isolation.
4. **AI's highest-leverage use for strategists is thinking, not producing.** Kevin frames the best use of AI tools as pressure-testing your own strategic assumptions, not just generating first drafts.
