## Title
Content Distribution in the Age of AI

## Source
YouTube — recorded live at "Zero Click" conference hosted by Profound, New York City

## Date
October 8, 2025 (published to YouTube November 13, 2025)

## Summary
Ross Simmonds delivers a tactical talk on how content distribution strategy must change as AI assistants (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews) become primary discovery surfaces. He argues that the "modern distribution stack" for any brand now includes: SEO foundations, organic and paid Reddit presence, YouTube strategy, review-site optimization (G2, Capterra, TrustRadius), and offsite mentions/digital PR — with the brand's own website treated as just one node in a larger system rather than the center of the strategy. He frames marketing as "distributed storytelling at scale," where repeated brand-and-category pairing across many surfaces is what trains AI systems to associate a brand with its space.

## Key Lessons
1. **Your website is one node, not the whole system.** AI assistants build their understanding of a brand from the cumulative web — review sites, communities, guest content, and press — not from a single optimized domain.
2. **The "modern distribution stack" is now a checklist, not a nice-to-have:** SEO, Reddit, YouTube, review platforms, and offsite mentions should each have a deliberate strategy and owner.
3. **Repetition builds AI association.** Consistent brand-and-category pairing across many independent sources is what "trains" AI tools to recommend a brand — a single great asset is not enough.
4. **Distribution decisions should be made at the content-planning stage**, not retrofitted after a piece is published.
