## Title
SEO in the Age of AI

## Source
RevOps FM Podcast (host: Justin Norris)

## Date
June 2025

## Summary
Gaetano DiNardi unpacks how AI has fundamentally changed SEO's competitive dynamics. He argues that classic SEO tactics — optimizing tags, improving page structure, building backlinks — are now simply "the cost of entry" rather than a source of competitive advantage, since virtually every serious competitor already does them; he calls the diminishing returns of widely-adopted tactics the "law of shitty clickthroughs." He explains "query fan-out" — where a single AI search prompt generates a web of synthetic sub-queries, producing a comprehensive AI-generated summary (pros, cons, pricing) that reduces the need to click through at all, with CTR drops of 30–70% observed on previously strong informational pages. He also notes that some AI systems are instructed not to cite sources for "timeless" or consensus-style queries (e.g., glossary/definition content), which disproportionately hurts cybersecurity and other vendors that have long relied on glossary pages for discovery. He also discusses how the same SEO playbook produces very different results for an incumbent brand versus a challenger, due to branded-signal weighting (direct searches, domain reputation, mention frequency).

## Key Lessons
1. **Foundational SEO tactics are now table stakes, not differentiators.** Backlinks, on-page optimization, and technical hygiene no longer create competitive separation because adoption is near-universal.
2. **Query fan-out can collapse click-through rates by 30–70%** on pages that used to drive strong informational traffic — SaaS teams need to identify which of their pages are most exposed to this risk and adjust content/monetization strategy accordingly.
3. **Glossary and "What is X" content is structurally disadvantaged in AI search** for timeless/consensus topics — vendors relying on this content type for top-of-funnel discovery (especially in technical categories like cybersecurity) need a new top-of-funnel strategy.
4. **Brand strength systematically distorts SEO playbook outcomes.** Challenger brands following the same tactical playbook as incumbents should expect structurally worse results due to branded-signal weighting — a critical expectation-setting point for SaaS marketing leaders reporting results to the board.
