## Title
How AEO / GEO / AI SEO Actually Works

## Source
Clearscope Webinar Series

## Date
Recorded December 11, 2025

## Summary
Bernard Huang presents Clearscope's internal AEO/GEO/AI-SEO playbook, framed around a clear premise: search traffic that has declined due to AI-driven answers is not coming back in its old form, so the strategic question is how to influence what AI assistants say about a brand rather than chasing traditional click volume. He breaks AI-driven search into three layers — **training data** (what the model learned during pretraining), **AI validation searches** (the real-time queries an AI runs to fact-check or ground its answer before responding), and **memory/context** (what an AI assistant retains about a brand across a session or user account). He argues the real goal of AEO/GEO is winning the citations that power AI answers, not maximizing pageviews.

## Key Lessons
1. **Search traffic decline is structural, not cyclical** — teams should stop benchmarking success against pre-AI traffic baselines and build new success metrics centered on citations and mentions.
2. **The three-layer model (training data, validation searches, memory/context) gives a complete systems view.** Most "AI SEO" advice only addresses the training-data layer; a complete strategy must also strengthen real-time validation surfaces (reviews, fresh content, structured data) and consider how repeat exposure builds AI "memory" of a brand.
3. **Winning citations is the new KPI**, replacing pure ranking position as the primary measure of search success for content strategy.
4. **AEO/GEO/AI SEO are functionally describing the same shift** — Bernard treats the terms as roughly interchangeable labels for one underlying discipline, which simplifies internal communication and avoids unnecessary jargon proliferation.
