How GEO Actually Works w/ Bernard Huang

## Summary
In a return appearance, Bernard digs into whether Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) is genuinely distinct from SEO or simply SEO evolving under new branding. The conversation covers how AI agents evaluate content differently from keyword-based SEO systems, the ongoing "traffic apocalypse" as AI Mode becomes the default search experience for a growing share of users, and the shift from counting backlinks to counting brand "mentions" in an LLM-mediated world. They also discuss practical measurement: impressions, citations, and branded search as the new core success metrics, and the strategic implications of the "long, long tail" — the idea that AI search may make extremely niche, specific queries newly viable to target since AI can synthesize answers for the long tail that previously had no dedicated content.

## Key Lessons
1. **"Good SEO is good GEO"** — most of what makes content successful in traditional SEO (depth, structure, credibility, intent match) directly transfers to AI-driven discovery; GEO is additive, not a wholesale replacement skill set.
2. **Mentions are replacing backlinks as the dominant authority signal** in an LLM-mediated world — link-building programs should evolve toward earning brand mentions across a wider range of surfaces, even where a traditional hyperlink isn't present.
3. **The "long, long tail" is newly addressable.** Because AI can synthesize answers to extremely specific, previously unservable queries, SaaS content teams have a new opportunity to capture very niche use-case and comparison queries that wouldn't have justified a dedicated page in the pre-AI search era.
4. **Top-of-funnel traffic compression is real and ongoing** — teams should expect continued informational-traffic decline as AI Mode becomes a default experience, and should weight content strategy more heavily toward mid- and bottom-funnel, decision-stage content where clicks and conversions still occur.
