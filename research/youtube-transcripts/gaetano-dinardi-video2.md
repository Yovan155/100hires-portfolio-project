## Title
Tough Questions CEOs Are Asking About AI SEO (and How To Answer Them)

## Source
Content and Conversation podcast — Siege Media (host: Ross Hudgens)

## Date
December 1, 2025

## Summary
Gaetano joins Ross Hudgens to address the increasingly difficult questions B2B SaaS CEOs are asking their marketing and SEO teams as AI reshapes search: Is traffic flat or down because of AI, or because of execution? Why does LLM-visibility data look so alarming on dashboards? Is SEO "still working" at all? The conversation works through how to separate genuine structural disruption from noisy, fear-driven dashboard data, and how marketing leaders should frame the SEO function's evolving role to skeptical executives — emphasizing that the function is changing shape (less raw click volume, more influence on AI-mediated decisions) rather than disappearing.

## Key Lessons
1. **Executives need a translated narrative, not raw dashboard data.** CEOs reacting to scary-looking AI-visibility dashboards need marketing leaders to contextualize the numbers (engine-specific, query-type-specific) before drawing conclusions about whether SEO is "working."
2. **Flat or declining traffic isn't automatically an AI problem.** Teams should rule out execution issues (content quality, technical health, competitive shifts) before attributing performance changes entirely to AI disruption.
3. **Attribution is getting messier, not non-existent.** The episode stresses building new measurement proxies (branded search lift, AI citation tracking, pipeline-stage influence) rather than abandoning measurement altogether.
4. **SEO's role is shifting from "traffic generator" to "trust and influence builder"** inside AI-mediated buying journeys — a framing CEOs need help understanding before they cut SEO budgets prematurely.
